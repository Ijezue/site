About me

I recently completed my Master’s in Computer Science at Texas Tech University, where I've developed a strong foundation in artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and cyber-physical systems. I also had the opportunity of working on benchmarking hard computing problems on quantum computers, under the guidance of Dr. Ziwen Pan. My current projects involve fine-tuning language models using Reinforcement Learning with an LLM as a judge, and developing AI agents.

My academic and research journey bridges software and hardware systems with real-world applications. I have an undergraduate degree in Mechatronics Engineering, where I began working on hardware-intensive projects. As a reference, for my undergraduate thesis, I designed and constructed a waste plastic bottle-picking UAV, combining engineering concepts and microcontroller programming. Furthermore, during my master’s degree, I have had the opportunity to work on projects and research that combine this practical foundation with computational depth, one of which is medical image classification using EMCAD [Efficient Multi-scale Convolutional Attention Decoding for Medical Image Segmentation].

On a personal note, I enjoy playing chess, working my way through the Wheel of Time book series, and experimenting with Canva templates.
